1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When the page opens, you are greeted with floating images. There's a stage in the middle that takes up most of the space and that is what first caught my attention. As I interacted with it, I was drawn into its subtle movement when the mouse hovers over the webpage.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

list

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Because I was intrigued by the mouse causing objects to move, I spent the most time clicking on objects to see where it redirects me and what appears. I went back and forth between pages and simply hovering my mouse to see if there is any correlation between the movements and exploring content is in the webpage.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Mouse movement based interactions and page redirection

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

Its an interaction base website that encourages users to be creative, and explore the website. Bringing “coding life” to the real world for new and innovative brands

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Its focused on “next gen” and that is correlated to the unusual and interactive/fun layout of the website. With 3D assets, floating motions and exploration based interactions. This enhances the experience encouraging creative thinking when navigating the website and shows that its talents are creative, innovative and iconic.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The experience could be interacted with over a short period of time. There are mainly three pages. Which is the main page, app pages, and the talents page. Users can explore this page within a couple of minutes but this could vary depending on how much users choose to experiment with the interactions

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Using 3D assets and minimal text. The layout of the website and its contents has minimal text, focusing on visuals and movement. This can reflect how it encourages users to navigate the website with a creative mind and continuous exploration

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

Mainly 3D modelling and UX design. Potentially colour understanding

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

I think to not assume what the intention of the website is and jump to conclusions. I almost didn't choose this website as the complicated navigation made me slightly not bothered to go further as I assumed it would be hard to understand the content and intention.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

To be open minded and less harsh on the websites layout

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

I think its the loading time between each refresh and redirection. I appreciate that its part of the experience but I dont have the patience for it.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

I guess that I have control over the website. How it moves is based on my mouse and it is satisfying to create the patterns and movement myself. As well as the colour coordination and pallet.
